37.

Mahatma Gandhi knew absolutely nothing about music. But when he said something about music, the whole world listened. He was a politician, but he became so famous that no matter what he spoke about, everyone took him seriously. Similarly, when Tagore said something, even if he knew nothing about that subject, others valued what he said. Why? Because he had become an extraordinary figure.1
