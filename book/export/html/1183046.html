He had a vision. According to him it was the highest vision that a human being can have. He was all excited. But the Master told him that that kind of vision is not of a high order, although it is encouraging and inspiring. The Master encouraged him to meditate more soulfully and devotedly and assured him that he would grant him a higher vision. But the disciple did not believe in the Masterâ€™s statement or assurance. He was positive that his vision was genuine and, at the same time, of a very high order, the highest order. Therefore, he left.
