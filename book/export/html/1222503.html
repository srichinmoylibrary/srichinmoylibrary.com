Some of My Best Friends Are Children

Some of my best friends are children.
In fact, all of my best friends are children.
[Words of J.D. Salinger]

If You Have One True Friend

If you have one true friend, you have more
than your share.
[Words of Thomas Fuller]

A Friend

A friend is one who dislikes
The same people you dislike.
[Anonymous]

Tell Me Who Admires and Loves You

Tell me who admires and loves you, and I will tell you who you are.
[Words of Charles Augustin Sainte-Beuve]

My Friends, There Are No Friends

My friends, there are no friends.
[Words of Coco Chanel]

He Who Is the Friend of All Humanity

He who is the friend of all humanity is not my friend.
[Words of Moliere]

Most People Enjoy

Most people enjoy the inferiority of their friends.
[Words of Lord Chesterfield]

One Friend in a Lifetime

One friend in a lifetime is much; two are many;
three are hardly possible.
[Words of Henry Adams]

To Find a Friend

To find a friend, one must close one eye:
To keep him, two.
[Words of Norman Douglas]

Friendship Is Like Money

Friendship is like money, easier made than kept.
[Words of Samuel Butler]

Instead of Loving Your Enemies

Instead of loving your enemies,
Treat your friends a little better.
[Words of Edgar Watson Howe]

Money Cannot Buy Friends

Money cannot buy friends, but you can get a better class of enemy.
[Words of Spike Milligan]

The Richer Your Friends

The richer your friends, the more they will cost you.
[Words of Elizabeth Marbury]

It Is Good to Have Some Friends

It is good to have some friends
Both in Heaven and in Hell.
[Words of George Herbert]

Wherever You Are

Wherever you are, it is your own friends who make your world.
[Words of William James]

From: Sri Chinmoy, Friendship songs, , 1993
