My Early Morning Prayer-Journeys, part 1

1.

My Lord,
Early in the morning
I love and worship
Your beautiful Eye
And beautiful Feet.

  1. EMP 1. Sanya, China, 5 December 2004↩

2.

My Lord,
During the day
I love and worship
Your powerful Eye
And powerful Feet.

  1. EMP 2. Sanya, China, 5 December 2004↩

3.

My Lord,
In the evening
I love and worship
Your peaceful Eye
And peaceful Feet.

  1. EMP 3. Sanya, China, 5 December 2004↩

4.

My Lord,
At night
I love and worship
Your merciful, Godful
And good-bye Eye
     And Feet.

  1. EMP 4. Sanya, China, 5 December 2004↩

5.

My Lord loves
     To whisper
All His Answers.

  1. EMP 5. Sanya, China, 6 December 2004↩

6.

Forgivers
Are immediate and everlasting
     Happiness-enjoyers.

  1. EMP 6. Sanya, China, 6 December 2004↩

7.

The heart-givers
Are the life-transformers
     Of the world.

  1. EMP 7. Sanya, China, 6 December 2004↩

8.

God does not want me
To count His Miracles.
He wants me to count
Only my God-love-heart-tears.

  1. EMP 8. Sanya, China, 6 December 2004↩

9.

When I sing,
My Lord smiles at me
     Lovingly.

  1. EMP 9. Sanya, China, 7 December 2004↩

10.

When I sing soulfully, My Lord blesses me Powerfully.

  1. EMP 10. Sanya, China, 7 December 2004↩

11.

When I sing soulfully,
Devotedly and self-givingly,
My Lord embraces me
     Immediately.

  1. EMP 11. Sanya, China, 7 December 2004↩

12.

The lower I bend my head
     To my Lord Supreme,
The deeper joy I receive
     From Him.

  1. EMP 12. Sanya, China, 7 December 2004↩

13.

The quicker I touch
The Lotus-Feet
Of my Lord Supreme,
The stronger I feel Him,
Physically and spiritually.

  1. EMP 13. Sanya, China, 7 December 2004↩

14.

I have
An eternal companion:
My heart’s inner poise.

  1. EMP 14. Xiamen, China, 8 December 2004↩

15.

Every morning
     I need
A newborn God-hunger.

  1. EMP 15. Xiamen, China, 8 December 2004↩

16.

My earthly music
     Is
My Heavenward journey.

  1. EMP 16. Xiamen, China, 8 December 2004↩

17.

My Heavenly music
Is the beauty
Of my universal life.

  1. EMP 17. Xiamen, China, 8 December 2004↩

18.

I sleeplessly adore
My highest height.

  1. EMP 18. Xiamen, China, 9 December 2004↩

19.

I breathlessly love
My deepest depth.

  1. EMP 19. Xiamen, China, 9 December 2004↩

20.

God,
With His Power,
     Comes down.

  1. EMP 20. Xiamen, China, 9 December 2004↩

21.

God,
With His Compassion,
     Lives on earth.

  1. EMP 21. Xiamen, China, 9 December 2004↩

22.

God,
With His Delight,
     Returns.

  1. EMP 22. Xiamen, China, 9 December 2004↩

23.

I must live
In my heart-home
At every moment.

  1. EMP 23. Xiamen, China, 10 December 2004↩

24.

My prayers must reach
The four corners
     Of the globe.

  1. EMP 24. Xiamen, China, 10 December 2004↩

25.

My meditations must intensify
The God-hunger
     Of every seeker.

  1. EMP 25. Xiamen, China, 10 December 2004↩

26.

I must offer my heart-tears,
Soul-smiles and life-gratitude
To my Lord Beloved Supreme
Unreservedly and unconditionally,
Every morning and every evening.

  1. EMP 26. Xiamen, China, 10 December 2004↩

27.

I do not pray to God
To make me a great man —
     No, no, no!

  1. EMP 27. Xiamen, China, 11 December 2004↩

28.

I do not pray to God
To make me a good man —
     No, no, no!

  1. EMP 28. Xiamen, China, 11 December 2004↩

29.

I pray to God
To be His, only His,
     All His.

  1. EMP 29. Xiamen, China, 11 December 2004↩

30.

I tell my Lord Beloved Supreme
That in every aspect of my life,
It is for Him to make
     The decision,
And I will take His Decision
As final, absolutely final.

  1. EMP 30. Xiamen, China, 11 December 2004↩

31.

This morning
I am a prayerful, soulful
     And grateful
God-obedience-heart.

  1. EMP 31. Xiamen, China, 12 December 2004↩

32.

The mind desires
Constant God-Attention.

  1. EMP 32. Xiamen, China, 12 December 2004↩

33.

The heart desires
Sleepless God-Affection.

  1. EMP 33. Xiamen, China, 12 December 2004↩

34.

My Lord Beloved Supreme
     And I
Thrive on our mutual fondness.

  1. EMP 34. Xiamen, China, 12 December 2004↩

35.

Every day
I am learning more and more
     The divine art
Of unconditional surrender
     To God’s Will.

  1. EMP 35. Xiamen, China, 13 December 2004↩

36.

Even for a fleeting moment,
The pangs of
My God-separation-heart
     Are unbearable.

  1. EMP 36. Xiamen, China, 13 December 2004↩

37.

With an inner thrill
I wait to receive
My Lord Supreme.

  1. EMP 37. Xiamen, China, 13 December 2004↩

38.

My heart-lotus
Blossoms only
At the Lotus-Feet
Of my Lord Supreme.

  1. EMP 38. Xiamen, China, 13 December 2004↩

39.

This morning
My Lord said to me,
“My child,
Doers accomplish;
Talkers perish.
I want you
To be a doer.”

  1. EMP 39. Xiamen, China, 15 December 2004↩

40.

I happily welcome
     My sweet days.
I bravely welcome
     My bitter days.

  1. EMP 40. Xiamen, China, 15 December 2004↩

41.

God says to all the seekers
     Of the world,
“Discipline your lives,
     My children,
Discipline your lives!
If you do not, I shall force you, I shall force you.”

  1. EMP 41. Xiamen, China, 15 December 2004↩

42.

My heart
Is made of
My Lord’s sweet Dreams.

  1. EMP 42. Xiamen, China, 15 December 2004↩

43.

Every day
I dive deeper and deeper
Into my Lord’s
Compassion-Ocean-Eye.

  1. EMP 43. Xiamen, China, 15 December 2004↩

44.

On Eternity’s Road,
One by one all may leave me,
But not my Lord’s
Compassion-Eye.

  1. EMP 44. Xiamen, China, 15 December 2004↩

45.

My Lord,
Not Power, not Light —
What I need from You
     Is Peace,
Your Infinity’s Peace.

  1. EMP 45. Xiamen, China, 15 December 2004↩

46.

I pray
In the temple of devotion
To receive Joy
From my Lord.

  1. EMP 46. Xiamen, China, 17 December 2004↩

47.

I meditate
In the temple of surrender
To receive Peace
From my Lord.

  1. EMP 47. Xiamen, China, 17 December 2004↩

48.

I love
My soaring heart-bird
     And not
My roaring mind-lion.

  1. EMP 48. Xiamen, China, 17 December 2004↩

49.

My mind sees
     The tearful God.
My heart sees
     The blissful God.

  1. EMP 49. Xiamen, China, 17 December 2004↩

50.

My love, my devotion
And my surrender
Have God-Blood-relations.

  1. EMP 50. Xiamen, China, 18 December 2004↩

51.

My Lord has been searching
For only one happy heart
     In His vast Creation.

  1. EMP 51. Xiamen, China, 18 December 2004↩

52.

A kind life
     Is
A God-chosen life.

  1. EMP 52. Xiamen, China, 18 December 2004↩

53.

A kind heart
     Is
A God-given heart.

  1. EMP 53. Xiamen, China, 18 December 2004↩

54.

May my heart
     Be a fountain
Of God-gratitude-tears.

  1. EMP 54. Xiamen, China, 19 December 2004↩

55.

May my life
     Be a sky
Of God-plenitude-smiles.

  1. EMP 55. Xiamen, China, 19 December 2004↩

56.

My Lord is already
Inside my heart
With His Compassion-Eye
To grant me God-realisation.

  1. EMP 56. Xiamen, China, 19 December 2004↩

57.

The Master sees his disciples
First inside his heart
And then in front of his eyes.

  1. EMP 57. Xiamen, China, 19 December 2004↩

58.

I pray to God
With my aspiration-eyes.

  1. EMP 58. Xiamen, China, 20 December 2004↩

59.

I meditate on God
With my aspiration-heart.

  1. EMP 59. Xiamen, China, 20 December 2004↩

60.

I walk and run
Towards God
With my aspiration-legs.

  1. EMP 60. Xiamen, China, 20 December 2004↩

61.

God has appointed
     My aspiration
To take me to Him
As soon as possible.

  1. EMP 61. Xiamen, China, 20 December 2004↩

62.

God wants me
To be the promoter
Of all the inspiring things
That are taking place
     Here on earth.

  1. EMP 62. Qingdao, China, 25 December 2004↩

63.

Quite often
The courage of the mind
Plays the role of an absentee.

  1. EMP 63. Qingdao, China, 25 December 2004↩

64.

My Lord gets
A shocking experience from me
When I do not adequately care for His Creation.

  1. EMP 64. Qingdao, China, 25 December 2004↩

65.

God always keeps me
Inside His Heart-Home
No matter how badly I fare
In my aspiration-life.

  1. EMP 65. Qingdao, China, 25 December 2004↩

66.

Beautiful
     Is
My heart-awakening.

  1. EMP 66. Qingdao, China, 26 December 2004↩

67.

Blissful
     Is
My God-remembrance.

  1. EMP 67. Qingdao, China, 26 December 2004↩

68.

Painful
     Is
My God-forgetfulness.

  1. EMP 68. Qingdao, China, 26 December 2004↩

69.

Our divine qualities
Brighten and gladden
     The Eye
Of our Lord Supreme.

  1. EMP 69. Qingdao, China, 26 December 2004↩

70.

My love, my devotion
     And my surrender
Will remain my unfailing friends
     Right to the very end.

  1. EMP 70. Qingdao, China, 28 December 2004↩

71.

What I badly need
Is a peace-flooded
Silence-heart.

  1. EMP 71. Qingdao, China, 28 December 2004↩

72.

World-harmony
And world-union
Remain the unrealised dreams
     Of our age.

  1. EMP 72. Qingdao, China, 28 December 2004↩

73.

I prayerfully welcome
     Every morning
The silence-arrival
Of my Lord Beloved Supreme
     At my heart-door.

  1. EMP 73. Qingdao, China, 28 December 2004↩

74.

My heart’s capacity
To feel God’s Compassion-Rain
Is far beyond the imagination
     Of my mind.

  1. EMP 74. Qingdao, China, 29 December 2004↩

75.

My Lord,
How is it that every time
I want to give You
Everything that I have
And everything that I am,
     I sadly fail?
“My child,
Develop more intensity
In your devotion to Me.”

  1. EMP 75. Qingdao, China, 29 December 2004↩

76.

O where can I get a passport
To enter into my Lord’s
Heart-Home-Land?

  1. EMP 76. Qingdao, China, 29 December 2004↩

77.

God tells me
That if I can silence my mind,
Then He will give me
     A free entry
Into all the countries
     In His Creation.

  1. EMP 77. Qingdao, China, 29 December 2004↩

Editor's preface to the first edition

During his trip to China from November 2004 to February 2005, Sri Chinmoy was inspired to offer a number of prayers very early in the morning, when he came down from his hotel room to go out walking. These 160 prayers are published in two volumes.

From: Sri Chinmoy, My Early Morning Prayer-Journeys, part 1, Agni Press, 2005
