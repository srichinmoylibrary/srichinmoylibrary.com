The Core Of India's Light, Part 2

1. Abhaya

Be fearless. Ignorance-tiger will immediately disappear.

2. Acharya

A spiritual Teacher is he who not only tells us and shows us where God is but also teaches us how we ourselves can eventually become God.

3. Aditya

The outer sun is inspiration.

The inner sun is at once aspiration and realisation.

4. Agni

If you constantly keep your inner fire burning, then God will be nearing you before long.

5. Aparajita

Everything is unconquerable except God. Once we conquer God with our unconditional surrender, we immediately see that the things that were unconquerable are now only unconquered, and the things that were unconquered are now being conquered. To conquer God’s Compassion is to conquer the conquerable. To conquer man’s stupidity is to conquer the unconquerable.

6. Aparicchinna

Infinite is the Goal. Eternal is the soul. We make friends with the Eternal in order to grow into the Infinite.

7. Aparoksha

In order to have the direct knowledge of God, try to unlearn immediately what you have learned so far from humanity’s stupidity, and learn once more what Divinity’s Reality has already taught you.

8. Apavitra

An impure thought lasting for a minute is strong enough to destroy a man’s aspiration for the rest of the day. A day lost is the precursor of a new defeat. A new defeat in the life of aspiration marks a fatal destruction.

9. Apurna

By doing something we cannot complete our incomplete life. By becoming something we cannot complete our incomplete life. But by establishing a cheerful and unconditional oneness with God’s Will we can easily complete our incomplete life.

10. Apurva

God the created universe is unique, and God the unmanifested Reality is unique. I need God the created universe to see God’s unique Face. I need God the unmanifested Reality to touch God’s unique Feet.

11. Archana

The body’s real offering to God is lethargy.

The vital’s real offering to God is aggression.

The mind’s real offering to God is doubt.

The heart’s real offering to God is insecurity.

The soul’s real offering to God is unwillingness to work in and through ignorance-night for God-manifestation.

12. Artha

With material wealth we are apt to challenge the world. With spiritual wealth we are apt to establish our inseparable oneness with God’s entire creation.

13. Asambhava

Impossibility at once starves our body’s necessity and feeds our soul’s challenging, illumining and fulfilling promise.

14. Asanga

Non-attachment is an illumining and liberating force. Attachment is a binding and blinding force.

Attachment-teacher tells us that we need everything in God’s creation. Non-attachment-teacher tells us that there is nothing on earth worth having.

15. Asanti

The absence of peace makes one feel that his is the life of an insignificant ant. The presence of peace makes one feel that his is the life for even God to be truly proud of.

16. Asara

When we do not aspire, our very life becomes barren. A barren life and God’s Compassion compete with each other. Eventually we see that God’s Compassion wins the race. And then what does it do? It immediately changes a barren life into the life of an aspiration-sun.

17. Asat

The easiest way to conquer fear, doubt and jealousy is not to say that they are destructive, but to convince the mind that they are totally non-existent.

18. Asmrti

Forgetfulness is a blessing when we dare to have a new and promising life.

Forgetfulness is a curse when we forget God’s Compassion-Light, which is our only fulfilling Satisfaction-Reality.

19. Asu

In the spiritual life, the breath of life is purity. And what does purity do? It expands the finite, and sets it in tune with the Infinite.

20. Asura

The worst possible demon is within us and not without us. The name of this demon is suspicion-night. This demon ruthlessly strangles us, kills us and destroys us.

21. Atanu

If we want to escape from the body’s prison and become part and parcel of the bodiless reality, then what we need is a constant friendship with the soul’s divinity-beauty.

22. Atindriya

As long as you are within the reach of the senses, you will try and cry. Once you are beyond the reach of the senses, you will fly and fly. But how can you be beyond the reach of the senses? You can be beyond the reach of the senses the moment you declare that your senses are for God to use, and not for you to use.

23. Atmabala

Self-giving is the matchless soul-force. This soul-force awakens the dormant human in us, liberates the bound divine in us and fulfils the unfulfilled God in us.

24. Atma-Drshti

Perfect perfection does not remain a far cry when we cultivate self-vision in everything and everyone.

25. Atma-Jnana

He who is blessed with the direct knowledge of the Transcendental Self is also blessed with the supreme Pride of the Absolute Supreme.

26. Atma-Prakasa

Self-revelation in the animal world is world-destruction. Self-revelation in the human world is ego-assertion. Self-revelation in the divine world is God-manifestation.

27. Atma-Trpti

Unless and until God-Satisfaction dawns in our aspiration-life, there can be no true self-satisfaction even for a fleeting second.

28. Avarana

How can we remove the veil of ignorance-night? We can do so only by becoming an untiring cry in the inner world and an unending smile in the outer world.

29. Baddha

God’s Grace has bound you; therefore, your presence is found in every aspiring and serving human being.

30. Bahudha

In many ways I can please God. One of the ways to please God is to stop spreading the expectation-net.

31. Bahya

Our external life needs simplicity and humility more than anything else. Our internal life needs sincerity and purity more than anything else.

32. Bandha

A bondage-thought instigates me to think that I must needs live only for my own existence-reality. A freedom-thought inspires me to feel that I lived, live and shall eternally live only for my Beloved Supreme.

33. Bhagavan

In the human world the Lord Supreme is the possession-king. In the divine world the Lord Supreme is the Perfection-Prince.

34. Bhajana

To love the real in us is to worship the Supreme in us. The real in us is the soul. The Supreme in us is self-realisation for God-manifestation.

35. Bhakti

Man thinks that God’s Compassion is sweeter than the sweetest. God knows that man’s devotion is sweeter than the sweetest.

36. Bhava

When we have a divine attitude towards our ideal Goal, we not only expedite our spiritual journey but also bring nearer to us the ideal Goal itself.

37. Bhaya

Fear is a strangled, divided reality which makes us feel that we can never claim the universal man and the transcendental God as our own, very own.

38. Bhiksha

To give alms to the needy is a sign of true generosity, and this generosity may help the receiver for some time. But the offering of an iota of soulful will from the depths of one’s heart can and will last for a very long time, if not forever.

39. Bhikshu

A mendicant roams from country to country to derive divine satisfaction from his God-search. A true seeker does not have to roam from country to country. His heart-home is large enough to house satisfaction supreme.

40. Bhoga

When we sing the song of enjoyment, we see that there are many things which we will never be able to enjoy. But when we dance the dance of renunciation, we see that there is nothing on earth which we cannot divinely enjoy. As a matter of fact, renunciation itself is supreme satisfaction.

41. Bhrashtah

In the spiritual life, if your aspiration descends, you are undoubtedly a fallen hero. But you need not remain a fallen hero if you have an iota of tearful gratitude for what you have already received from your previous inner oneness with the higher realities that came from your inner cry.

42. Bhranti

To say that you love God more than He loves you is to deliberately enjoy the worst possible stupid mistake, both in your desire-life and in your aspiration-life.

43. Bija

Yesterday I was the seed of inspiration. Today I am the plant of aspiration. Tomorrow I shall not only be the tree of realisation but also the fruit of man-satisfaction in God.

44. Bhumi

Earth is great because of its patience-heart. Heaven is great because of its compassion-soul.

45. Bhuvah

The higher world longs for the manifestation of God-Illumination. The lower world cries for the descent of God-Compassion.

46. Bhuh

On the earth plane uncertainty reigns supreme. The manifestation of truth is uncertain. Everything is uncertain on the earth plane, save and except God’s Compassion.

47. Bhuma

The Delight of the Infinite the finite can never achieve but can easily receive, provided it becomes the song of constant selflessness.

48. Bhuvana

Who can change the world? Not he who just talks. Not he who just does. Not he who says and does. Not he who does and says. But he who eternally is.

49. Brahma

Brahma created the world. If I can change my own thought-world, then the Creator of the world will give me what I do not have: satisfaction in self-giving and satisfaction in God-receiving.

50. Brhat

Large is man’s heart. Larger is man’s promise. Largest is man’s gratitude-oneness with God’s Will.

51. Chaitanya

Consciousness binds the aspiration-cry of the earth-reality with the Satisfaction-Smile of the Heaven-Divinity.

52. Chakra

Each centre of psychic power reveals the natural capacities of the inner world in a tangible form on the human plane.

53. Chakshu

The third eye sees, liberates and fulfils. The third eye sees the past, the present and the future. It illumines the aspiring human being in us and satisfies the serving divine being in us.

54. Chanchala

For the restless mind, Infinity’s peace will always remain a far cry.

55. Cheshta

Each time we endeavour to see good in mankind and do good for mankind, we become not only God’s better instruments but also His confidants.

56. Chidananda

The flood of consciousness-delight can be achieved only by the seeker’s unreserved heart and unconditional life.

57. Chinta

An anxiety-stricken person delays the moment of illumination-poise and accelerates the hour of destruction-noise.

58. Daiva

Fate can be changed only when we knock at God’s Compassion-Gate and not at our desire’s gratification-gate.

59. Daksha

In order to become an expert in any walk of life, we must needs work very hard. We also must know that no sincere effort will be in vain. Each and every sincere effort will be crowned with success, of course, at God’s own choice Hour.

60. Dama

Control your senses. You will not only feel the existence of ecstasy-world but also grow into the very breath of ecstasy-world.

From: Sri Chinmoy, The Core Of India's Light, Part 2, Agni Press, 1992
