My Early Morning Prayer-Journeys, part 2

1.

No more, no more,
This year’s failure-days
And failure-nights —
No more, no more!

  1. EMP 78. Qingdao, China, 31 December 2004↩

2.

No more,
The faith-starvation-days
And faith-starvation-nights
Of this year —
No more!

  1. EMP 79. Qingdao, China, 31 December 2004↩

3.

The old year painfully
Goes to sleep.

  1. EMP 80. Qingdao, China, 31 December 2004↩

4.

The new year bravely
Wakes up.

  1. EMP 81. Qingdao, China, 31 December 2004↩

5.

Happy New Year,
Happy New Year!
Be brave, be brave!
No fear, no fear!

  1. EMP 82. Qingdao, China, 1 January 2005↩

6.

Happy New Year,
Happy New Year!
Go forward,
Fly upward,
Dive inward!
No fear, no fear!

  1. EMP 83. Qingdao, China, 1 January 2005↩

7.

Happy New Year!
God the Eye you claim,
God the Heart you claim
And God the Life you claim.
Claim, claim, claim, claim God.
No fear, no fear!

  1. EMP 84. Qingdao, China, 1 January 2005↩

8.

Happy New Year,
Happy New Year!
I shall be my God-hope-joy,
I shall be my God-promise-fulfiller.
No fear, no fear!

  1. EMP 85. Qingdao, China, 1 January 2005↩

9.

I proudly declare
My greatness-mind —
     Achievements.

  1. EMP 86. Qingdao, China, 2 January 2005↩

10.

My Lord proudly uses
My goodness-heart —
     Achievements.

  1. EMP 87. Qingdao, China, 2 January 2005↩

11.

My mind-education
Is an accurate
Knowledge-fact-collector.

  1. EMP 88. Qingdao, China, 2 January 2005↩

12.

My heart-education
Is the collector
Of God’s Heart-Tears
And God’s Eye-Smiles.

  1. EMP 89. Qingdao, China, 2 January 2005↩

13.

Every morning I sing
A God-worshipping
Prayer-song.

  1. EMP 90. Qingdao, China, 2 January 2005↩

14.

An unseen Hand
Compassionately regulates
     My life.

  1. EMP 91. Qingdao, China, 3 January 2005↩

15.

Silence
Is the Mother
Of world harmony.

  1. EMP 92. Qingdao, China, 3 January 2005↩

16.

When I am
An indomitable will,
God’s Thrill
Is unimaginable.

  1. EMP 93. Qingdao, China, 3 January 2005↩

17.

Today God is so pleased
With my prayers and meditations
That He has blessingfully
     Offered me
His golden Crown.

  1. EMP 94. Qingdao, China, 3 January 2005↩

18.

In the small hours of the morning,
God and I enter into
A lovely competition.
     My task is
To touch God’s Feet.
     God’s Task is
To examine my heart.
I finish my task long before God.
Poor God accepts the sad defeat.

When the night is far advanced,
God and I again enter into
Another lovely competition.
This time our task is
Mutual appreciation.
I shall have to appreciate God
As much as I can.
God will have to appreciate me
As much as He wants to.
Alas, I accept the very sad defeat.

  1. EMP 95. Qingdao, China, 3 January 2005↩

19.

My silence-heart
Gladdens my Lord Supreme
Beyond measure.

  1. EMP 96. Qingdao, China, 5 January 2005↩

20.

God’s Silence-Eye
Frightens me
To the core.

  1. EMP 97. Qingdao, China, 5 January 2005↩

21.

My mind,
Stop covering my heart-sky
With your thick and dark clouds!

  1. EMP 98. Qingdao, China, 5 January 2005↩

22.

My heart,
God is right in front of you.
Where is your attention,
Where?

  1. EMP 99. Qingdao, China, 5 January 2005↩

23.

God wants my heart
To be the owner
Of His Dream.

  1. EMP 100. Qingdao, China, 5 January 2005↩

24.

My Lord,
Do make me a bond-slave
To Your Will.

  1. EMP 101. Qingdao, China, 6 January 2005↩

25.

Alas, I do not know
When I will be able to return
To my Lord’s Heart-Home.

  1. EMP 102. Qingdao, China, 6 January 2005↩

26.

A path-finder
     Is
A new God-discoverer.

  1. EMP 103. Qingdao, China, 6 January 2005↩

27.

To manifest God
As perfectly as possible:
This is what my heart and I
Precisely stand for.

  1. EMP 104. Qingdao, China, 6 January 2005↩

28.

Our Lord’s Compassion-Eye
     Protects us
From the visible enemies.

  1. EMP 105. Qingdao, China, 7 January 2005↩

29.

Our Lord’s Concern-Heart
     Protects us
From the invisible enemies.

  1. EMP 106. Qingdao, China, 7 January 2005↩

30.

My Lord breathes in
My heart-flower-fragrance.

  1. EMP 107. Qingdao, China, 7 January 2005↩

31.

A God-oneness-heart-dream
     Is
Sweetness unimaginable.

  1. EMP 108. Qingdao, China, 7 January 2005↩

32.

My God-obedience
     Is
My supremely divine pride.

  1. EMP 109. Qingdao, China, 8 January 2005↩

33.

I live either inside
My earth-born heart-cries
     Or inside
My Heaven-free soul-smiles.

  1. EMP 110. Qingdao, China, 8 January 2005↩

34.

God wants me
Not only to equal Heaven,
But also to surpass Heaven
In sleepless self-offering.

  1. EMP 111. Qingdao, China, 8 January 2005↩

35.

The soul perpetually smiles
Through the bondage-cage
     Of the body.

  1. EMP 112. Qingdao, China, 8 January 2005↩

36.

Every day
I wish to have
An enthusiasm-outburst.

  1. EMP 113. Qingdao, China, 9 January 2005↩

37.

Alas,
We try to satisfy
Our Lord Supreme
With our extremely feeble efforts.

  1. EMP 114. Qingdao, China, 9 January 2005↩

38.

In my aspiration-heart-home
There is no room
For uncertainty.

  1. EMP 115. Qingdao, China, 9 January 2005↩

39.

There was a time
When I used to live
On the outskirts of hope,
But now I am living
In the very breath of hope.

  1. EMP 116. Qingdao, China, 9 January 2005↩

40.

Even the most powerful
Ignorance-night
Shall be illumined
By our inner light.

  1. EMP 117. Qingdao, China, 10 January 2005↩

41.

Humanity’s hope-flames
Rise to Heaven.

  1. EMP 118. Qingdao, China, 10 January 2005↩

42.

Divinity’s promise-stars
Touch Mother Earth.

  1. EMP 119. Qingdao, China, 10 January 2005↩

43.

From the oneness-heart
     We arrive
At the newness-shore.

  1. EMP 120. Qingdao, China, 10 January 2005↩

44.

From the newness-shore
     We arrive
At the fulness-shore —
The Golden Shore.

  1. EMP 121. Qingdao, China, 10 January 2005↩

45.

Life is meant
For our God-union
     And not
For our mind-division.

  1. EMP 122. Xian, China, 14 January 2005↩

46.

God-discovery-song
Tops the list
Of all our love, devotion
And surrender-songs.

  1. EMP 123. Xian, China, 14 January 2005↩

47.

Our uniqueness comes
Either from God’s Feet
Or from God’s Heart.

  1. EMP 124. Xian, China, 14 January 2005↩

48.

I must find myself
In the heart of time eternal
And in the heart of space infinite.

  1. EMP 125. Xian, China, 14 January 2005↩

49.

Those who outwardly
And indiscriminately display
An orchestra of spiritual power
Are not favoured
Either by Heaven’s Eye
Or by earth’s heart.

  1. EMP 126. Xian, China, 16 January 2005↩

50.

Nature
Is the divine beauty
Of God’s supreme Glory.

  1. EMP 127. Xian, China, 16 January 2005↩

51.

An act of compassion
Is one step forward
Towards God-realisation.

  1. EMP 128. Xian, China, 16 January 2005↩

52.

The morning touch
Makes my heart beautiful.

  1. EMP 129. Xian, China, 16 January 2005↩

53.

The evening touch
Makes my life peaceful.

  1. EMP 130. Xian, China, 16 January 2005↩

54.

My God-surrender-life
Is the birth
Of a new creation.

  1. EMP 131. Xian, China, 16 January 2005↩

55.

My heart-sun
     Feeds on
God’s Transcendental Delight.

  1. EMP 132. Xian, China, 16 January 2005↩

56.

A new heart is needed
To love goodness
In every human being.

  1. EMP 133. Xian, China, 17 January 2005↩

57.

O my mind,
You must not remain a stranger
To God’s Heart
Any more.

  1. EMP 134. Xian, China, 17 January 2005↩

58.

World-criticism
Has no place
In my aspiration-heart
And in my dedication-life.

  1. EMP 135. Xian, China, 17 January 2005↩

59.

My heart tells my eyes
That I am very near
My Lord’s Heart-Home.

  1. EMP 136. Xian, China, 17 January 2005↩

60.

God is asking us
Only for two things:
For our prayers to be sincere
     And
For our meditations to be deep.

  1. EMP 137. Xian, China, 19 January 2005↩

61.

The heart
Readily and immediately listens
     To the dictates
Of the Inner Pilot.

  1. EMP 138. Xian, China, 19 January 2005↩

62.

God heavily and proudly
Counts on our self-offering.

  1. EMP 139. Xian, China, 19 January 2005↩

63.

I am eager to go
To the four corners of the world
To find a man
Who is constantly happy,
Cheerful and self-giving.

  1. EMP 140. Xian, China, 19 January 2005↩

64.

May my aspiration
Have another name:
     Fragrance.

  1. EMP 141. Xian, China, 21 January 2005↩

65.

May my dedication
Have another name:
     Cheerfulness.

  1. EMP 142. Xian, China, 21 January 2005↩

66.

May my prayer
Have another name:
     Soulfulness.

  1. EMP 143. Xian, China, 21 January 2005↩

67.

May my meditation
Have another name:
Intensity.

  1. EMP 144. Xian, China, 21 January 2005↩

68.

May I
Have another name:
     Integrity.

  1. EMP 145. Xian, China, 21 January 2005↩

If we really love God,
Then our heart-smiles should be
Spontaneous and continuous.

  1. EMP 146. Huangshan, China, 25 January 2005↩

70.

It is high time for me
To say good-bye
To each and every thing
That I do not need,
Such as my desire-life.

  1. EMP 147. Huangshan, China, 25 January 2005↩

71.

The real spiritual life
Does not mean
To keep your eyes closed
All the time
And do nothing,
Nothing for the world.

  1. EMP 148. Huangshan, China, 25 January 2005↩

72.

I must keep
Each and every thought
Of my mind
Absolutely silent
While I meditate.

  1. EMP 149. Huangshan, China, 25 January 2005↩

73.

The sun-duty
Is calling me.
I am running!

  1. EMP 150. Huangshan, China, 31 January 2005↩

74.

The moon-beauty
Is calling me.
I am running!

  1. EMP 151. Huangshan, China, 31 January 2005↩

The star-frolic-joy
Is calling me.
I am running!

  1. EMP 152. Huangshan, China, 31 January 2005↩

76.

My life lives between
A body-camel-burden
And
A heart-deer-speed.

  1. EMP 153. Huangshan, China, 31 January 2005↩

77.

My protection-fort
     Is made of
My God-obedience
     And
My God-dependence.

  1. EMP 154. Nanjing, China, 3 February 2005↩

78.

My Lord,
May I have a heart
Of ever-blossoming smile.

  1. EMP 155. Nanjing, China, 3 February 2005↩

79.

There is a God-loving
And God-serving
Divine hero
Within every heart.

  1. EMP 156. Nanjing, China, 5 February 2005↩

80.

Even an iota
Of consciousness-descent
In the spiritual life
Is a grave failure.

  1. EMP 157. Nanjing, China, 6 February 2005↩

Every morning

A heart-to-heart talk
Before I enter into
The hustle and bustle of life.

  1. EMP 158. Nanjing, China, 7 February 2005↩

82.

Each God-dream
Is eager to be
A manifested Reality.

  1. EMP 159. Nanjing, China, 8 February 2005↩

83.

My Lord,

     Only on
Your Compassion-Smiles?

Editor's preface to the first edition

During his trip to China, from November 2004 to February 2005, Sri Chinmoy was inspired to offer a number of prayers very early in the morning, when he came down from his hotel room to go out walking. These 160 prayers are published in two volumes.

From: Sri Chinmoy, My Early Morning Prayer-Journeys, part 2, Agni Press, 2005
