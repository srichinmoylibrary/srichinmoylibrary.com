18615

The simplicity of life
We were.
The sincerity of the mind
We were.
The purity of the heart
We were.
The divinity of our prayers
We were.
Alas, we do not know
Whether we have deserted them
Or they have deserted us.

18616

Without my heart's hope
I cannot proceed.
But without my mind's expectation
I can easily proceed,
And I must,
For expectation and frustration
Are immediate neighbours.

18617

My morning prayer
Cries for God's Compassion.
My evening prayer
Longs for God's Satisfaction.

18618

When I bow my head
In humble prayer,
My Lord Supreme makes me
Taller than the tallest
And helps me shine
Inside His own Heart-Garden.

18619

Dynamism-plants
Will never befriend us
If we do not cheerfully
And bravely
Uproot our lethargy-trees.

18620

Alas, is there any desire-man
Who can ever avoid
Being a puppet
In the hands of inexorable fate?

18621

In our aspiration-heart's
Dedication-nest,
No stranger
There can ever be.

18622

The life of a saint
Is indeed
The Heart-Fragrance
Of God Himself.

18623

I do not care for
My mind's determination-success.
All I need is
My heart's aspiration-progress.

18624

Alas, alas, when I pray
The outer world remains aloof.
When I meditate
The inner world remains blind.

18625

Only the unaspiring mind
Can enjoy the recital
Of worthless and useless thoughts.

18626

I fully agree with those
Who say that the curious mind
Is not the proper place
For prayer.

18627

When my mind surrenders
To my heart's fragrance-delight,
I enjoy Divinity's Delight.

18628

My Lord Supreme,
Do give me the capacity
To keep my heart-room
Always available
For You, only for You.

18629

The mind
And its hesitation
Together are destroying
The heart
And its aspiration.
