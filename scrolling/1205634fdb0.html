19860

The aspiration-stairways
Are meant only for
The self-giving confidence-hearts.

19861

What is my mind?
I wish I never knew.
What is my heart?
I wish to immediately know.

19862

To perpetuate
Our aspiration and dedication,
We need two things:
A purity-heart
And
A sincerity-mind.

19863

We cannot conquer God's Heart
With our mind's smartness.
We can conquer God's Heart
Only with our heart's soulfulness.

19864

I can, at best,
Think of God fondly.
But God can easily
Think of me self-givingly.

19865

Today's wisdom can erase
Yesterday's follies,
Provided we value
Our higher-than-the-highest Goal.

19866

O my heart's sweetness,
You do have the capacity
To save my mind and its dryness
With your compassion-light.

19867

God blessed me
With His Compassion-Fortune.
Alas, my stupid mind
Has squandered it.

19868

God never comes to visit
My mind-palace,
But He frequently comes to visit
My heart-cottage.

19869

O my insincere mind,
Your spirituality-practice
Not only amuses me
But also embarrasses me.

19870

O my endless desire-mind,
Are you not afraid
Of your forthcoming
Desire-death sentence?

19871

God smiles and smiles
When I entertain Him.
God cries and cries
When I try to ascertain
The extent of
His Compassion for me.

19872

If aspiration
Is not available,
How can realisation
Be available?

19873

Before, God and I
Were very close to each other.
Now that I have withdrawn my love,
Poor God finds it impossible
To be close to my heart.

19874

I prefer
God's Justice-Hand
To man's compassion-eye.
